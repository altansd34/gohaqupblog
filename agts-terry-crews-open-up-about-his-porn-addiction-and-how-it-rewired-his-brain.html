    America’s Got Talent host, Terry Crews recently was Live on Instagram and he shared his childhood memories with pornography. The TV star gave an informative broadcast from his home in Santa Clarita on the hidden dangers behind online pornography and how it affected him at such a young age.

    Terry Crews’ Plea To The Quarantined People Watching Porn

    While people all over the world are quarantined, the rise in pornography has been widely reported. One of the most popular porn sites PornHub is actually offering free preniums upgrades to the people in Italy (Wonder what the Pope thinks about that?). Although it is seen as a healthy outlet for plenty of adults, that is not the case for Terry Crews and he wants to share that with people. In this very detailed video below, the AGT host opens up about his addiction and the impact it had on his brain and his marriage.

    This is also not the first time Crews has opened up about his addiction that “rewired” his brain.

    Terry Crews’ Long Time Struggle With Pornography Addiction

    Ever since Terry Crews confessed to his wife about his addiction to pornography, he has spoken openly online about its dangers. The TV star even appeared on the Dr. Phil show to elaborate on his years of addiction to pornography and how it nearly ended his marriage. Now, as the masses are stuck inside due to the ongoing Coronavirus epidemic and more people are turning to pornography for a dopamine hit, Crews took to Instagram Live to share his story. 

    “I notice that people are using porn as a way to get through this”, begins Crews. ‘What is porn? What is it about? It’s when you are basically using an image or images of other people, human beings and treating them like your personal objects”, he continued.

    America’s Got Talent host then went into detail on the common confusion between sex and love. ‘What’s wild is that so many people now have confused sex with love. Love, however, has concern for the other person.” Crews made it clear he doesn’t agree with banning pornography all together but believes that “you need all the information” first. 

    Crews shares his first experiences with pornography at a young age

    “I was exposed at a very young age”, says Crews as he begins to detail his first experiences with pornography. He goes on to say, “I didn’t even know what sex was. I was totally blown away. The images take over your whole mind, it was rewiring my whole brain”.

    Terry then explains that the pornographic magazines that he was mesmerized by as a child, were easier to regulate when it came to who read them. Now, with online pornography sites across the internet, requiring no age confirmation, it poses a threat to the young generation. “Porn is trying to get people under 18. Most people I know have been exposed at a very young age”. America’s Got Talent host then compares the way pornography works to the drug dealers within his hometown of Flint, Michigan. He says that the drug dealers of his town would give out crack cocaine for free, knowing they will return once hooked. Crews says pornography websites act in a similar way.
