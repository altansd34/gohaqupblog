Why the United Kingdom’s deal with Indiana is worth watching On May 27, the United Kingdom inked a trade agreement with the state of Indiana. The deal is unlikely to be an economic blockbuster: It is nonbinding, covers only 1.8% of U.S. GDP and is narrowly focused on quasi-regulatory issues — like mutual recognition of certain professional certifications, academic cooperation and state-level procurement — that fall under states’ legal jurisdiction. Tariff reductions, historically the bread and butter of trade agreements, are therefore off the table.
